# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers lead the design of user interfaces and user experiences in collaboration with Product Managers and Developers. They ensure products are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Produce wireframes, mockups, and prototypes
- Conduct usability testing and gather user feedback
- Ensure accessibility standards and best practices are followed
- Coordinate design feedback with Product Managers and Developers
- Participate in design reviews and iterate on solutions

### Goals
- Deliver user-centered, intuitive experiences
- Reduce friction in user workflows
- Ensure brand consistency and accessibility compliance

### Typical Communication
- Design review sessions with stakeholders
- Collaboration with Developers on implementation feasibility
- User research findings and feedback loops
- Design specifications and design systems documentation

### Interactions
- Works closely with Product Managers for user goals and requirements
- Collaborates with Developers for implementation and technical constraints
- Partners with QA for testing and usability validation
- Engages with stakeholders for design feedback and approval

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers plan and coordinate release and deployment activities, ensuring quality, schedule adherence, and minimal risk to production systems.

### Responsibilities
- Organize and maintain release timelines and schedules
- Prepare comprehensive release notes and documentation
- Manage go/no-go decisions and deployment windows
- Communicate releases to stakeholders, support, and end users
- Track deployment metrics and post-release verification
- Coordinate rollback plans and incident response

### Goals
- Deliver releases on schedule with zero critical incidents
- Maintain clear communication across all stakeholders
- Minimize deployment risk and unplanned downtime

### Typical Communication
- Release planning and coordination meetings
- Deployment checklists and verification reports
- Stakeholder release announcements
- Post-deployment status reports

### Interactions
- Collaborates with Project Managers for release schedules and priorities
- Works with QA for validation and sign-off
- Partners with Developers for technical release steps and dependencies
- Coordinates with Support Lead for handover and incident readiness
- Communicates with stakeholders on release status and timelines

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts define and analyze business and functional requirements, acting as a critical bridge between stakeholders and delivery teams to ensure alignment and clarity.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, document, and clarify business and functional requirements
- Model business processes and workflows
- Create requirement specifications and acceptance criteria
- Support user acceptance testing (UAT) activities
- Identify and communicate process improvements and risks
- Facilitate communication between business and technical teams

### Goals
- Ensure requirements are clear, complete, and testable
- Bridge the gap between business needs and technical solutions
- Reduce rework and misalignment during delivery

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Functional specifications and process documentation
- Requirement traceability matrices and impact analysis
- UAT support and defect reporting

### Interactions
- Facilitates between stakeholders and the delivery team
- Collaborates with Product Managers and Project Managers on requirements and scope
- Works with Developers to ensure technical feasibility
- Partners with QA for test scenario development
- Engages with Support Lead for operational requirements

---

## Support Lead

### Role Summary
Support Leads coordinate the transition of products to operational support, manage incident response, and facilitate feedback loops from production to the delivery team.

### Responsibilities
- Prepare handover documentation and operational runbooks
- Triage and prioritize incidents and support tickets
- Escalate unresolved issues to appropriate teams for investigation
- Communicate with end users and coordinate support responses
- Collect and relay customer feedback to Product and Development teams
- Contribute to post-incident reviews and process improvements

### Goals
- Ensure customer satisfaction and system reliability
- Minimize mean time to resolution (MTTR) for incidents
- Provide actionable insights for product improvements

### Typical Communication
- Support handover documentation and playbooks
- Incident and issue reports with root cause analysis
- Customer feedback summaries and feature requests
- Collaboration with development teams on bug reproduction and fixes

### Interactions
- Partners with Project Managers and Release Managers for deployment readiness
- Works with Developers for root cause analysis and bug fixes
- Collaborates with QA for bug reproduction and validation
- Engages with Product Managers on feature requests and improvements
- Communicates with end users and escalation contacts

---

## Security Champion

### Role Summary
Security Champions advocate for secure development practices and help identify, manage, and remediate security risks throughout the entire project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct threat modeling and security architecture reviews
- Review code and designs for security vulnerabilities
- Ensure compliance with security standards and regulations
- Raise security awareness and best practices within the team
- Participate in incident response and forensics for security events
- Track and manage security risks in the risk register

### Goals
- Build security into every phase of development
- Reduce security vulnerabilities and compliance violations
- Foster a security-conscious culture within the team

### Typical Communication
- Security review meetings and threat modeling sessions
- Vulnerability reports and remediation recommendations
- Security training and awareness initiatives
- Security risk updates and incident reports

### Interactions
- Partners with Developers for secure coding practices and implementation
- Collaborates with QA for security testing and validation
- Consults with Project Managers on security risks for the risk register
- Engages with Release Manager on security validation before deployment
- Coordinates with external Security and Compliance teams as needed
