## OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome! This section provides a summary of how we manage projects at OctoAcme and links to all detailed process documentation.

### Overview of Project Management Processes

OctoAcme projects follow an iterative, customer-focused, risk-aware approach:

**Project Lifecycle & Workflows:** OctoAcme follows a structured five-phase project lifecycle: Initiation, Planning, Execution, Release, and Close & Retrospective. During Initiation, teams validate the business need through a lightweight Project One-pager that captures the problem statement, success metrics, stakeholders, and key risks. Once approved, Planning breaks work into shippable increments with prioritized backlogs and clearly defined Definitions of Done. Execution follows an iterative delivery model with daily standups, weekly delivery syncs, and a project board structure that provides visibility into work status. The Release phase emphasizes pre-deployment verification through smoke tests and security scans, followed by staged rollout to production. Finally, retrospectives capture learnings and convert them into actionable improvements.

**Roles & Organizational Structure:** OctoAcme operates with clear role delineation to ensure ownership and accountability. Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. Product Managers own the product vision, define what should be built, and measure outcomes. Developers implement features, write tests, and participate in code reviews. QA/Testing validates quality against acceptance criteria. This structure is supported by a cadence of weekly PM-PdM syncs, twice-weekly standups, and monthly stakeholder updates.

**Communication & Risk Management:** OctoAcme emphasizes transparency and proactive risk management. A Risk Register tracks risks by impact, likelihood, owner, and mitigation status, reviewed weekly during syncs. Three-level escalation paths enable team-level triage, PM escalation to Product Leads, and Sponsor involvement for business-impacting issues. Status communication follows a standard template covering progress, next steps, risks, and decisions needed.

**Quality Assurance & Delivery Standards:** Quality is embedded throughout the delivery process. Teams enforce small, reviewable Pull Requests (≤400 lines), automated testing and linting in CI/CD, and require peer approval before merging. Quality gates include unit tests, integration tests, end-to-end smoke tests, and security scanning. Post-deployment verification and observability dashboards ensure ongoing reliability.

### Getting Started

**New to OctoAcme?** Start with the [Project Management Overview](docs/octoacme-project-management-overview.md) to understand our principles and key roles.

**Starting a new project?** Follow the [Project Initiation Guide](docs/octoacme-project-initiation.md) and then the [Project Planning](docs/octoacme-project-planning.md) guide.

**Running a project?** Refer to [Execution & Tracking](docs/octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md) and [Risk Management & Communication](docs/octoacme-risks-and-communication.md) throughout delivery.

**Preparing to release?** Check the [Release & Deployment Guide](docs/octoacme-release-and-deployment.md).

**Finishing a project?** Schedule a retrospective using [Retrospective & Continuous Improvement](docs/octoacme-retrospective-and-continuous-improvement.md).

### Key Principles

- **Customer-first:** Prioritize customer value and usability.
- **Iterative delivery:** Deliver small, testable increments.
- **Clear ownership:** Each project has named Project Manager and Product Lead.
- **Data-informed decisions:** Measure impact and iterate based on evidence.
- **Psychological safety:** Encourage feedback and learning.

Refer to these guides to ensure your projects stay aligned with our best practices.
